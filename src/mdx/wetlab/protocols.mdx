      <DropdownItem value="ph-acid-baths" header="Preparation of Acid Baths (pH 1.5, 2.0, 3.0)" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Clean glassware
        - DI water
        - 50 mL tubes / small flasks
        - Analytical balance
        - 0.2% NaCl solution (2.0 g/L → 0.06 g/30 mL)
        - 1 M HCl
        - pH meter (calibrated at 4/7/10)
        - 37 °C water bath

        ###### Methods
        1. Add 0.06 g NaCl to ~25 mL DI water.  
        2. Add HCl (approximate volumes):  
           - pH 1.5 → 0.57 mL  
           - pH 2.0 → 0.18 mL  
           - pH 3.0 → 18 µL  
        3. Bring to 30 mL with DI water.  
        4. Check and adjust pH.  
        5. Warm to 37 °C before use.  
      </DropdownItem>

      {/* Plaque Assay General */}
      <DropdownItem value="plaque-assay-general" header="General Phage Plaque Assay (Double-Agar Overlay)" level={1}>
       

        ###### Purpose
        Standard techniques for quantifying bacteriophage titers (PFU/mL) and testing phage activity on bacterial lawns.

        ---

        ###### Materials
        - LBa plates (1.5% agar)
        - Soft/top agar (0.4–0.7% agar, molten at 55 °C)
        - Host bacteria culture (OD₆₀₀ = 0.4–0.6)
        - LB broth
        - Sterile pipette tips, microtubes
        - Phage stock solutions
        - Water bath (55 °C)
        - Sterile spreaders
        - 37 °C incubator

        ---

        ###### Methods

        **A. Plate & Bacterial Preparation**
        1. Prepare LBa plates (1.5% agar).  
        2. Melt soft/top agar (0.4–0.7%) and equilibrate to 55 °C before use.  
        3. Mix 100 µL host bacteria with ~5 mL molten top agar and pour evenly.  
        4. Let solidify for ~5 min.  

        **B. Phage Serial Dilutions**
        1. Dispense 180 µL LB into each dilution tube.  
        2. Add 20 µL phage stock to the first tube (10⁻¹).  
        3. Transfer 20 µL sequentially up to 10⁻⁶.  
        4. Mix and change tips each time.  

        **C. Spotting**
        1. Apply ~4 µL of each dilution to the agar surface.  
        2. Do 3 replicates per dilution.  
        3. Let dry, then incubate inverted at 37 °C overnight.  

        **D. Readout (Spot Analysis)**
        1. Count visible plaques.  
        2. Calculate PFU/mL using:  
           PFU/mL = (number of plaques × dilution factor) / volume of phage dilution (mL)  
        3. Record plaque morphology (size, clarity).  

        ---

        

        **Troubleshooting**  
        - Too few plaques → mix 100 µL bacteria + 100 µL phage.  
        - Fully cleared plate → increase dilution factor.  
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="plaque-assay-stability" header="Plaque Assays for Capsule & Free-Phage Stability" level={1}>
        ###### Purpose
        Quantify phage survival and release kinetics following exposure to different pH conditions.

        ---

        ###### Materials
        - Host culture at OD₆₀₀ = 0.4–0.6
        - LB broth, soft/top agar (0.4–0.7%)
        - Capsule and free-phage bath samples
        - Sterile tubes and tips
        - LBa plates (1.5% agar)
        - 37 °C incubator

        ---

        ###### Methods

        **A. Serial Dilutions**
        | Sample Type | Dilution Range |
        |--------------|----------------|
        | Release (pH 6.8–7.4) | 10⁻⁴ – 10⁻⁷ |
        | Release (pH 6.0) | 10⁻³ – 10⁻⁶ |
        | Acid-hold | 10⁻² – 10⁻⁵ |
        | Free-phage acid | neat – 10⁻² |
        | Free-phage neutral | 10⁻⁴ – 10⁻⁶ |

        1. Use 180 µL LB per dilution, transfer 20 µL each step.  
        2. Mix thoroughly.  

        **B. Plating**
        1. Mix 100 µL of each dilution with 100 µL host bacteria.  
        2. Add to ~5 mL molten top agar (55 °C) and pour.  
        3. Solidify (~5 min), incubate inverted at 37 °C overnight.  

        ---

        ###### Next Day – Readout
        1. Count plates with 30–300 plaques.  
        2. Calculate PFU/mL for each condition.  
        3. Determine % survival vs input control.  
        4. Report:
           - Capsule integrity vs pH (% intact at 120 min).  
           - Median release time (≥ pH 6.0).  
           - PFU survival after release vs free-phage controls.
