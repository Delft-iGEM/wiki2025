    <Dropdown header="Pouring LB Agar Plates">
      ### Materials
      - Petri dishes
      - 400 mL LB agar medium
      - Water bath at 100 °C
      - Water bath at 60 °C
      - 400 µL kanamycin (50 mg/mL stock), pipette tips
      - 70% ethanol
      - Bunsen burner

      ### Methods
      1. **Melt agar:** Place LB agar in a 100 °C water bath until fully liquefied.
         Transfer to a 60 °C water bath and cool until pourable (not solidified).
      2. **Set up sterile field:** Label each plate (date, antibiotic, "iGEM"). Sterilize bench
         with 70% ethanol. Create a sterile area with a Bunsen burner; stage plates, molten agar, and kanamycin near the flame.
      3. **Add antibiotic:** To **400 mL** molten LB agar add **400 µL**
         kanamycin stock (50 mg/mL) → final **50 µg/mL**. Mix thoroughly.
      4. **Pour plates:** Under flame, pour ~**20 mL** per dish to cover the bottom.
         Close lids and let solidify on the bench.
    </Dropdown>
