    <summary><strong>Module C</strong></summary>

    <details>
      <summary><strong>Pouring LB Agar Plates</strong></summary>

      <h3>Materials</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Petri dishes</li>
        <li>400 mL LB agar medium</li>
        <li>Water bath at 100 °C</li>
        <li>Water bath at 60 °C</li>
        <li>400 µL kanamycin (50 mg/mL stock), pipette tips</li>
        <li>70% ethanol</li>
        <li>Bunsen burner</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Methods</h3>
      <ol>
        <li>
          <strong>Melt agar:</strong> Place LB agar in a 100 °C water bath until fully liquefied.
          Transfer to a 60 °C water bath and cool until pourable (not solidified).
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Set up sterile field:</strong> Label each plate (date, antibiotic, “iGEM”). Sterilize bench
          with 70% ethanol. Create a sterile area with a Bunsen burner; stage plates, molten agar, and kanamycin near the flame.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Add antibiotic:</strong> To <strong>400 mL</strong> molten LB agar add <strong>400 µL</strong>
          kanamycin stock (50 mg/mL) → final <strong>50 µg/mL</strong>. Mix thoroughly.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Pour plates:</strong> Under flame, pour ~<strong>20 mL</strong> per dish to cover the bottom.
          Close lids and let solidify on the bench.
        </li>
      </ol>
    </details>

  </details>
