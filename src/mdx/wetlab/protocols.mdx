    <DropdownGroup multiple className="mt-4">
      <DropdownItem value="ph-acid-baths" header="Preparation of Acid Baths (pH 1.5, 2.0, 3.0)" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Clean glassware
        - DI water
        - 50 mL tubes / small flasks
        - Analytical balance
        - 0.2% NaCl solution (2.0 g/L → 0.06 g/30 mL)
        - 1 M HCl
        - pH meter (calibrated at 4/7/10)
        - 37 °C water bath
        - 0.22 µm filter (if storing overnight)

        ###### Methods
        1. **Weigh & dissolve:** Add **0.06 g NaCl** to ~**25 mL DI water**.
        2. **Add HCl (approximate volumes):**  
           - pH **1.5** → **0.57 mL**  
           - pH **2.0** → **0.18 mL**  
           - pH **3.0** → **18 µL**  
        3. **Adjust volume:** Bring final to **30 mL** with DI water.  
        4. **Check pH:** Fine-tune if necessary.  
        5. **Warm:** Place at **37 °C**, re-check pH before use.  
        6. **Sterilize:** Filter (**0.22 µm**) if storing overnight.  
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="ph-neutral-basic-baths" header="Preparation of Neutral/Basic Baths (pH 6.0, 6.8, 7.4)" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Clean glassware
        - DI water
        - 50 mL tubes / bottles
        - 0.5 M KH₂PO₄ (acid stock)
        - 0.5 M K₂HPO₄ (base stock)
        - pH meter (calibrated at 4/7/10)
        - 37 °C water bath
        - 0.22 µm filter (prepare in advance)

        ###### Methods
        1. **Combine phosphate stocks (per 30 mL, 0.05 M buffer):**  
           - pH **6.0** → **0.175 mL K₂HPO₄** + **2.825 mL KH₂PO₄**  
           - pH **6.8** → **0.84 mL K₂HPO₄** + **2.16 mL KH₂PO₄**  
           - pH **7.4** → **1.825 mL K₂HPO₄** + **1.175 mL KH₂PO₄**  
        2. Add ~**25 mL DI water**, mix gently.  
        3. **Check pH:** Adjust with drops of acid/base stock as needed.  
        4. **Adjust volume:** Bring to **30 mL** with DI water.  
        5. **Warm:** Place at **37 °C**, re-check pH before use.  
        6. **Sterilize:** Filter (**0.22 µm**) if storing overnight.  
      </DropdownItem>
    </DropdownGroup>

      <DropdownItem value="lb-agar-plates" header="Pouring LB Agar Plates" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Petri dishes
        - 400 mL LB agar medium
        - Water bath at 100 °C
        - Water bath at 60 °C
        - 400 µL kanamycin (50 mg/mL stock), pipette tips
        - 70% ethanol
        - Bunsen burner

        ###### Methods
        1. **Melt agar:** Liquefy at **100 °C**, then hold at **60 °C** until pourable.
        2. **Sterile field:** Label plates; clean bench with **70% EtOH**; work by flame.
        3. **Add antibiotic:** Add **400 µL** of **50 mg/mL** kanamycin to **400 mL** agar → **50 µg/mL** final; mix.
        4. **Pour plates:** ~**20 mL** per dish; close lids; let solidify.
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="dna-resuspension" header="Resuspension of Dried DNA (Plasmid)" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Ordered DNA plasmid (tube)
        - Centrifuge
        - Milli-Q water
        - Spectrophotometer / Nanodrop

        ###### Methods
        1. Spin at **13,000 rpm for 20 s** to collect DNA.
        2. Add **20 µL** Milli-Q to the bottom; flick to wet/dissolve.
        3. Incubate **RT, 2 h**; flick again.
        4. Verify concentration (**~50 ng/µL**) by spectrophotometer.
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="transformation" header="Plasmid Transformation into BL21(DE3)" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - LB + Kan (50 µg/mL) agar plates
        - 2 mL tubes
        - **40 µL** BL21(DE3) competent cells
        - **1.6 µL** plasmid DNA
        - **770 µL** SOC
        - 42 °C water bath, 37 °C incubator/shaker

        ###### Methods
        1. On ice, mix **40 µL** cells + **1.6 µL** plasmid; incubate **30 min**.
        2. Heat-shock **42 °C, 30 s**; place on ice **5 min**.
        3. Add **770 µL** SOC; shake **37 °C, 150 rpm, 60 min**.
        4. Plate **10/20/50/100 µL** and include a negative control.
        5. Incubate inverted **37 °C, 16 h**.
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="glycerol-stock" header="Preparation of a Glycerol Stock (50% working)" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - **25 mL** 95% glycerol solution
        - **31.5 mL** Milli-Q water
        - **50 mL** flask

        ###### Methods
        1. Mix to make **56.5 mL** of **50%** glycerol.
        2. Transfer to a flask, label with autoclave tape, **autoclave**.
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="kanamycin-stock" header="Preparation of 1000× Kanamycin Stock (50 mg/mL)" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Kanamycin sulfate powder
        - Sterile Milli-Q water
        - 0.22 µm syringe filter
        - Sterile 50 mL tube

        ###### Methods
        1. Dissolve **250 mg** kanamycin sulfate to **50 mL** (→ **50 mg/mL**).
        2. Filter-sterilize (**0.22 µm**) into a sterile tube.
        3. Aliquot (e.g., **3 mL**), label; **store −20 °C**.
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="iptg-induction" header="Induction with IPTG" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - **50 mL** overnight culture (BL21(DE3) + MS2 VLP plasmid)
        - **1 L** LB + **1 mL** 1000× kanamycin
        - **1 M** IPTG
        - 2.5 L baffled flask, shaker/incubator
        - OD₆₀₀ spectrophotometer & cuvettes

        ###### Methods
        1. In a **2.5 L** flask add **10 mL** ON culture; bring to **1 L** LB+Kan.
        2. Grow **30 °C, 150 rpm** for **2–4 h**.
        3. Monitor **OD₆₀₀** to **0.5**; remove **50 mL** for *uninduced control*.
        4. Add **0.4 mL** **1 M IPTG**; incubate **17 °C, 150 rpm, 16 h** (control same conditions without IPTG).
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="harvest-lysis" header="Harvest and Lysis" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - **1 L** induced + **1 L** uninduced cultures
        - **2 L** LB (no antibiotic)
        - Large centrifuge bottles, 300 mL & 50 mL tubes
        - **1 mM NaNO₃**, pH 8 (≥500 mL)
        - PMSF (100 mM), Lysozyme (**10 mg/sample**)
        - 0.22 µm filters, sonicator, ice
        - SDS-PAGE loading dye

        ###### Methods
        1. Spin two rounds per culture: fill to **750 mL** with LB, **4000×g, 20 min, 4 °C**; discard supernatant.
        2. Save **50 mL** supernatant from each (store **4 °C**).
        3. Resuspend pellets in **200 mL** NaNO₃; adjust to **300 mL**.
        4. Add **10 mg** lysozyme; **30 min RT**. Add **PMSF to 0.3 mM**.
        5. **Sonicate** **3 min** (10 s on/20 s off) at **~50 W**.
        6. Take **50 mL** total lysate from each; high-speed spin later; keep remainder **4 °C**.
        7. **13,500×g, 20 min, 4 °C**; collect supernatant and **0.22 µm** filter.
        8. Resuspend pellets in **50 mL** NaNO₃; store fractions **4 °C**.

        | 4    | Control total lysate             | 10 µL + 2.5 µL dye   |
        | 5    | Induced pellet                   | 10 µL + 2.5 µL dye   |
        | 6    | Control pellet                   | 10 µL + 2.5 µL dye   |
        | 7    | Induced pre-lysis supernatant    | 10 µL + 2.5 µL dye   |
        | 8    | Control pre-lysis supernatant    | 10 µL + 2.5 µL dye   |
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="sds-page-gel" header="Loading & Running SDS-PAGE Gel" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Precast SDS-PAGE gel (e.g., Bio-Rad 4568021)
        - Electrophoresis cell
        - 1× Tris-Glycine-SDS running buffer
        - Protein ladder

        ###### Methods
        1. Mount gel; remove bottom tape; assemble in gel box.

      <DropdownItem value="coomassie-staining" header="Coomassie Staining & Imaging" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Coomassie Brilliant Blue R-250 stain
        - Destain (MeOH/AcOH/H₂O)
        - Tray/rocker
        - Imaging system

        ###### Methods
        1. Stain **1–2 h** (or overnight) with gentle rocking.
        2. Destain, changing solution every **30–60 min** until background clears (**~2–6 h**).
        3. Rinse **15–30 min** in water; image; store gel hydrated at **4 °C**.
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="peg-precipitation" header="PEG Precipitation of MS2 VLPs" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - PEG-6000, NaCl
        - 0.45 µm & 0.22 µm filters
        - Centrifuge (≥10,000×g)
        - Fresh buffer (e.g., **400 mM NaNO₃**)

        ###### Methods
        1. Filter VLP suspension through **0.45 µm**.
        2. Add PEG/NaCl to **10% PEG** + **0.5 M NaCl**  
           *(for 30 mL: 3 g PEG 6000 + 0.88 g NaCl)*.
        3. Incubate **4 °C, 18 h**; spin **10,000×g, 1 h, 4 °C**.
        4. Resuspend pellet in **1/10** initial volume buffer; filter **0.22 µm**; store **4 °C**.
      </DropdownItem>

      <DropdownItem value="tem-imaging" header="TEM Imaging of MS2 VLPs" level={1}>
        ###### Materials
        - Induced & uninduced VLPs
        - Carbon-coated copper grids (400-mesh)
        - Milli-Q water; **1–2%** uranyl acetate (negative stain)
        - Glow-discharge unit
        - TEM (80–120 kV), tweezers, dust-free drying box

        ###### Grid Preparation
        1. Glow-discharge grids **60 s** in air; store dust-free.

        ###### Sample & Stain
        1. Place **3 µL** sample on Parafilm; place grid (carbon side down) **60 s**; blot.
        2. Float on **10 µL** **2%** uranyl acetate **60 s**; wick; air-dry.
        3. Repeat for uninduced sample.

        ###### Imaging
        1. Load grid; operate TEM at **~100 kV** (bright-field).
        2. Optimize apertures & astigmatism; focus on thin stain film.
        3. Capture overviews (**10–20k×**) and details (**50–100k×**) for both samples.
      </DropdownItem>
